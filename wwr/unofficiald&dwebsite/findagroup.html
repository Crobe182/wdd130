Finding a Group

In-Person Groups

For those who prefer face-to-face games, local game stores, community centers, and libraries often host beginner-friendly D&D sessions. You can also check Meetup.com for gaming events in your area or ask around at board game cafes. Many players find that playing in person allows for more natural roleplaying and table interactions, making it a great option for new adventurers.

Online Groups

If you're looking to play Dungeons & Dragons online, there are plenty of platforms to help you connect with other players. Websites like Roll20, StartPlaying, and D&D Beyond's LFG forums allow you to find and join virtual games easily. Many of these platforms also offer matchmaking tools and beginner-friendly groups. Virtual tabletops like Foundry VTT, Fantasy Grounds, and Owlbear Rodeo provide digital maps, dice rollers, and character sheets to make online play immersive and fun.

Popular Options

Some of the most popular ways to find D&D groups are through social media and online communities. Platforms like Reddit's r/LFG, Discord servers, and Facebook groups are full of players looking for new members. Whether you're interested in joining an existing campaign or starting your own, these communities are a great way to connect with other fans of the game.
